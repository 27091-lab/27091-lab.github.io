The Problem

AI can now create fake videos, voices, and images that look real. These “deepfakes” can be used to spread misinformation, scam people, or damage reputations.

Most users cannot tell what is real anymore—and companies are not required to clearly disclose AI-generated content.

The Law

  • All AI-generated content must be labeled clearly
  • Deepfakes must include visible or audible watermarks
  • Users must be told when interacting with AI systems
  • Strict penalties for harmful or deceptive AI use
  • High-risk AI must pass safety and bias checks

Who Benefits

  • Citizens – protection from scams and fake media
  • Students – safer and more trustworthy information
  • Businesses – increased consumer trust
  • Creators – protection from impersonation

Concerns & Criticism

  • May slow innovation in AI development
  • Could increase costs for small companies
  • Technology may evolve faster than regulation

Why Support This Law?

Without action, AI can be used to manipulate elections, spread fake news, and harm individuals.

This law ensures transparency, accountability, and trust—while still allowing innovation to continue responsibly.
